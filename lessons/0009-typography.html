Design Taste/ Module C: Craft/ Lesson 09/ ~15 min

Typography: the loudest single decision

Your one win

By the end you can name a typeface's category and voice on sight, keep display and body in their lanes, and state the serif rule: when a serif is earned, and why Instrument Serif and Fraunces are "banned as default". The font is the first thing anyone reads before they read a word.

Change nothing but the font and the whole page changes its mind. Before a reader parses a single word, the letterforms have already said cheap or considered, technical or literary, loud or quiet.

So the goal is not "pick a nice font". It is to hear the voice a face is using, know which voices belong in a headline versus a paragraph, and stop reaching for the one the tool reaches for by reflex.

Fonts have voices

One word, six faces

The same word, set in six typefaces. Nothing changes but the letterforms, yet each one arrives in a different tone. Read the category and the vibe under each until you can hear it before you name it.

Tracking and leading, by hand

Two dials decide whether type feels tuned or default: tracking (space between letters) and leading (space between lines). Drag them. Notice where each one helps and where it hurts.

Tighten the display,
breathe the body.

Tight tracking earns its place on big display type; on body it jams the letters together and slows reading. Leading is the mirror image: body wants more (around 1.5), display wants less (near 1.0). Push both to the extremes and watch it break.

The italic-descender clip

A real bug you will see constantly: an italic heading in a box with a tight line-height and overflow: hidden. The slanted descenders (the tails on g, y, p) get sheared off. The fix is not a smaller font, it is room: enough leading and a little padding.

How to emphasize one word

When you want a word to stand out in a headline, lean on the same family's italic. Do not drop a serif word into a sans headline to make it feel special: two voices collide and it reads as a mistake, not emphasis.

Display versus body, and the serif rule

Display

Big, seen not read: headings, heroes. Character is welcome, tracking can be tight, leading near 1.0. Cabinet Grotesk and Anton live here. A display face at 15px in a paragraph is a tell.

Body

Small, actually read: paragraphs, UI. Wants a calm workhorse, normal tracking, leading around 1.5. Inter, Geist, Satoshi live here. This is where restraint reads as quality.

The serif rule. A serif is earned when the content is genuinely editorial: long-form writing, a literary or luxury brand, a magazine voice. It is not a shortcut to "premium". Instrument Serif and Fraunces are banned as defaults precisely because every AI mock reaches for them to fake sophistication, so they now signal the opposite. Earn the serif or use a grotesk.

Name the face

Recognition first: read the letterforms and name the typeface. The specimen is the same test word each time, so only the voice is doing the talking.

Pick the pairing

Then judgment: a good pairing gives each role a distinct voice that still belongs to the same world. A bad one either clashes or uses one face for two jobs.
