Design Taste/ Module C: Craft/ Lesson 10/ ~15 min

Color: neutrals do the work, one accent gets the glory

Your one win

By the end you can name a palette family on sight, state the color rules as rules (one accent lock, a saturation ceiling, warm-or-cool gray discipline, no pure black or white), and recognize the banned AI "premium" default by its hexes. Most of a page is neutral. The color you notice is doing a job.

A palette is not a mood board of colors you like. It is a small set of tokens, and most of them are neutral. The surface, the ink, the hairlines: all grays. One accent, used sparingly, carries the personality. Change the values, keep the roles, and the same page becomes a different brand.

So "having taste in color" is mostly restraint plus a few hard rules. Lock to one accent. Keep saturation off the ceiling. Pick a gray temperature and stay in it. Never reach for pure black or white. And know the one warm palette every AI defaults to, so you can refuse it on sight.

The rules, stated plainly

One accent lock

One accent color, used sparingly on the things that act: a primary button, a link, a live badge. A second accent competes and the page loses its center. Neutrals carry everything else.

Saturation ceiling

Keep the accent below roughly 80% saturation. Full-tilt neon reads as a template default; a slightly muted hue reads as chosen. The eye trusts restraint.

No pure black or white

Ink is an off-black with a hint of the substrate's temperature; the page is an off-white, not #FFFFFF. Pure #000 on pure #FFF is harsh and, again, a default.

Tinted shadows

Shadows are not gray. Tint them toward the substrate or the ink so they sit in the same light as everything else. A pure-black shadow floats.

Spot the violation

Four small palettes, each breaking exactly one rule. Read the chips, then name the rule it breaks. The fix is always the rule stated in reverse.
