Design Taste/ Module B: Naming/ Lesson 08/ ~15 min

Pattern Glossary II: motion names

Your one win

By the end you can watch a page behave and name the motion: staggered reveal, sticky-stack, pin/scrub, marquee, magnetic button, spring versus linear. Plus the two rules that separate real motion from AI motion: motion must be motivated, and it must degrade when someone asks for less of it.

Style is what a page is made of. Layout is how it is arranged. Motion is how it behaves over time, and it is the one layer you cannot screenshot. You name it by watching.

Every demo below is live and looping. Two of them (sticky-stack and pin/scrub) ask you to scroll inside the frame; the magnetic button wants your cursor. Drive them. A motion pattern is a behaviour, and you only really hold the word once you have felt the thing move.

Six motion behaviours

The vocabulary of movement

These are the moves a good site actually uses. Name the one you want and you can ask an AI for that behaviour instead of accepting a page that either sits dead still or fades everything in at once.

M1Staggered Reveal

Elements enter one after another on a small delay, not all at once. Draws the eye down in reading order. The single most useful entrance.

M2Sticky-Stack

Cards pin at the top and pile up as you scroll, each holding under the last. Turns a plain list into a deck you move through.

M3Pin / Scrub

M6Spring vs Linear

The easing: linear moves at one machine-flat speed; spring accelerates, overshoots, settles. Spring reads as physical, linear reads as robotic.

Feel the easing

Easing is the difference between motion that feels designed and motion that feels like a progress bar. Play the runner, then flip it to spring: same distance, same duration, completely different character. The overshoot is the whole tell.

Motion must be motivated

The rule that kills most AI motion: every movement should carry meaning, direct attention, or show a relationship. Decoration that just wobbles is noise, and noise is the fastest way to make a serious page feel cheap. Same content, two treatments.

And it must degrade

Some people get motion sick, and their browser says so with prefers-reduced-motion. Good motion has a resting state to fall back to: the page still reads, nothing is mid-fade, no information was carried by movement alone. Flip the switch and watch every demo above settle into the layout a reduced-motion visitor sees. If a demo went blank, it was built wrong.
