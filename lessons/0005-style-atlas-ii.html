The one everyone fakes: glassmorphism

"Frosted glass" is the most-attempted and most-botched dark effect. The failure is always the same: people reach for backdrop-filter: blur() and stop there. Blur alone has no edge, so the card reads as a flat grey smudge. Real glass has a lit rim: a 1px inner border plus a bright inner highlight, so light appears to bend at the edge. That rim is the whole illusion.

Right

Refraction edge

Blur + 1px white/16 border + inner highlight. The rim catches light; the card feels like a pane.

blur + hairline border + inset highlight

Wrong

Blur only

No border, no highlight. Same blur, but the edges dissolve and it reads as a muddy grey box.

blur only, no edge treatment

Why "liquid glass" is a claim to check

Apple documents Liquid Glass for its own platforms. On the web there is no official liquid-glass.css: every web version is an approximation with backdrop-filter and layered borders. So when a brief or a tool says "use Apple's liquid glass", the honest move is to build the approximation and label it as one, and to ship a solid-fill fallback for prefers-reduced-transparency. Glass that ignores that setting is inaccessible.

Telling the dark families apart

  • Ethereal Glass vs Deep Dark Mode: both dark, but Glass lives on pure OLED black with colored orbs and frosted cards. Deep Dark is graphite with solid bordered cards and no blur. If you can see through a card, it is Glass.
  • Dark Tech vs Tactical Telemetry: both mono and neon-on-black. Dark Tech is a terminal (prompt, cursor, command you type). Telemetry is an instrument (readouts, coordinates, status you monitor). Hacker types; telemetry watches.
  • Bold Studio Solid vs everyone: it is the only one that is not dark. A single saturated color fills the whole field. If the background is a confident color instead of black or white, you are in Studio Solid.

A quiet warning about black

Ethereal Glass uses near-OLED #050505, not pure #000000. Even at maximum darkness the families avoid true black, because pure black kills depth and reads as a digital default. Darkness is a color choice, not the absence of one.
