Design Taste/ Module A: Seeing/ Lesson 01/ ~12 min

The Tell: what AI slop actually looks like

Your one win

By the end you can look at a generated landing page and name five specific tells. Those are the exact signatures that make a design read as machine-made rather than designed.

A tell, borrowed from poker, is an involuntary signal that gives you away. AI-generated interfaces have tells: not "bad taste" in the abstract, but a small set of concrete, repeatable defaults the model reaches for when it tries to look designed. Taste starts with being able to see them. You cannot fix, or direct a tool away from, a pattern you cannot name.

Who this is for

This is lesson 1 of a 16-lesson course to make you fluent in design vocabulary, so you can steer AI tools (and your own hands) toward work that does not look generic. No design background needed. Full syllabus in the README.

Now hunt the hidden ten

The five pins you read were the loud, visual tells. But ten more tells are hiding in the left panel: in the brand name, the copy, the labels, the numbers, and the footer. They're the quiet ones, and they're where most generated pages actually give themselves away.

Click anything in the left panel you suspect is a tell. A hit turns green and names itself; a miss just says "no tell here." Try to find all ten before you hit Reveal remaining.

Hidden tells found: 0 / 10 Look past the pretty parts. Read the words, the names, the numbers.

The five you should be able to name from memory

Fluency is retrieving without looking. Here are the five loud tells again as a bare list. Cover the reasons and see if you can say why each one reads as generated:

  1. Inter, everywhere. The default typeface of AI design. Character-neutral, so it makes everything feel templated.
  2. The purple/blue gradient glow. The single most common AI fingerprint. The corpus calls avoiding it the Lila rule.
  3. Centered headline over a dark mesh. The one hero composition the model always reaches for first.
  4. Three equal feature cards. The generic feature row. Icon, bold word, one sentence, times three.
  5. The em-dash as a flourish. The model's signature punctuation crutch, banned outright in the corpus.

If you can name those five cold, you've hit today's win. The full taxonomy (every tell in this lesson plus the ones coming in later lessons) lives on your printable cheat sheet.
