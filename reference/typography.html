Categories · how to sort any face on sight

Grotesque
Early sans, a little industrial and characterful (Geist, Cabinet Grotesk). Slightly irregular, warmer than neo-grotesque.
Neo-grotesque
The refined, neutral descendants (Inter, Helvetica). Even and quiet, engineered to disappear. The safe body baseline.
Geometric grotesk
Built from near-circles and straight lines (Satoshi, Futura lineage). Friendly, modern, a touch warmer.
Display face
Made to be seen big, not read small (Anton, Cabinet Grotesk at size). Character and tight tracking welcome; unusable as body.
Editorial / display serif
High-contrast serifs with a literary voice (Instrument Serif, Fraunces). Earned in long-form and luxury; a tell as a default.
Monospace
Every glyph the same width (JetBrains Mono, Geist Mono). Reads as code, data, technical. Pairs with its own family or a matching sans.

Disciplines · the rules that keep type tuned

  • Display vs body. Display is seen (headings): character welcome, tracking can be tight, leading near 1.0. Body is read (paragraphs): calm workhorse, normal tracking, leading around 1.5. A display face at 15px in a paragraph is a tell.
  • Tracking earns its place on big type only. Tight letter-spacing suits large display; on body it closes the letters up and hurts reading.
  • Leading runs opposite to size. Small body text wants more line-height (~1.5); large display wants less (~1.0).
  • Emphasize with the same family's italic. Never inject a serif word into a sans headline; two voices read as a mistake, not emphasis.
  • Give italics room. A tight line-box with overflow:hidden shears italic descenders. Fix with leading and a little padding, not a smaller size.
  • The serif rule. A serif is earned by genuinely editorial content, not used as a shortcut to "premium". Instrument Serif and Fraunces are banned as defaults because every AI mock reaches for them, so they now signal the opposite.
  • Tabular figures for numbers in columns. Use tabular (monospaced) figures so digits align in tables, prices, and dashboards.
