The Pattern Glossary

Layout patterns you can point an AI at by name. Read each as a silhouette: dark bars are headlines, thin bars are body text, the hatched panel is an asset, the red block is a CTA. Heroes, grids, and composition anchors from Lesson 07. Motion patterns arrive in Lesson 08.

Hero paradigms · the shape of the first screen

H1Asymmetric Split

Text one side, asset the other, generous whitespace. The workhorse hero for SaaS and premium consumer.

H2Editorial Manifesto

Huge type, no asset, almost a poster. The one time a centered hero is earned: launches and editorial.

FORM

H3Video / Media Mask

Type cut out as a mask over a video or image. Cinematic and heavy; needs a real asset behind it.

typeMOVEStype

H4Kinetic-Type

Animated typography is the primary visual. The words perform; empty without real motion behind it.

H5Curtain-Reveal

The hero parts on scroll like a curtain. A scroll-driven entrance, not a static layout.

H6Scroll-Pinned

The hero stays pinned while the content scrolls past behind it. Holds one message while the story moves.

Layout & grid families · how the middle is arranged

L1Bento Grid

Asymmetric tiles of mixed sizes (Apple Control Center). Rhythm, not six identical cards.

L2Masonry Layout

Staggered grid, no fixed row height. Tiles pack by height. Bento aligns to a grid; masonry does not.

↑↓

L3Split-Screen Scroll

Two halves moving in opposite directions on scroll. One holds while the other advances, or they counter-scroll.

L4Zigzag (alternating)

Image-left/text-right, then flipped. Capped at two in a row: the third (flagged) is a pre-flight fail.

  • Bento cell-count rule. A bento has exactly as many cells as you have content for. An empty tile in the middle or at the end means the grid was planned wrong. Reshape it; never paste a blank to balance the grid.
  • Zigzag alternation cap. Two alternating image-and-text rows is fine. The third in a row is banal. Break the run with a full-width section, a bento, or a vertical stack.

Composition anchors · where the text sits in a section

Inside one section, the text block can anchor in ten places. Reflex puts it left-of-image every time. Vary the anchor and use at least three across a page.

1. Centered statement

2. Top-left lead, support bottom-right

3. Bottom-left text over image

4. Bottom-right CTA cluster

5. Left-third caption, right visual

6. Right-third caption, left visual

7. Centered low, text in lower band

8. Off-grid editorial offset

9. Stacked center, ultra-minimal

10. Image-as-canvas, text in a safe area
