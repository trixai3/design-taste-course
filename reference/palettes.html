Rules · the discipline that keeps a palette tuned

  • One accent lock. One accent color, used sparingly on the things that act (primary button, link, live badge). A second accent competes and the page loses its center.
  • Saturation ceiling. Keep the accent below roughly 80% saturation. Full neon reads as a template default; a slightly muted hue reads as chosen.
  • Neutrals do the work. Surface, ink, and hairlines are all grays. Most of the page is neutral so the one accent has somewhere to land.
  • Pick a gray temperature. Every gray leans warm or cool. Mixing the two reads as off without a nameable reason. Choose one and keep all grays in it.
  • No pure black or white. Ink is an off-black tinted toward the substrate; the page is an off-white. Pure #000 on #FFF is harsh and, again, a default.
  • Tinted shadows. Shadows carry a hint of the substrate or ink color, not pure gray, so they sit in the same light as everything else.
  • Refuse the beige-brass-espresso default. The AI "premium" reflex. If a brief pulls you there, commit harder (dark ground) or pick a specific accent instead.
