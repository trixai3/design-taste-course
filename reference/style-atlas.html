The Style Atlas

Named style families you can point an AI at. Read a family in three questions: substrate, hierarchy carrier, edge signature. Light families from Lesson 04; dark and bold families arrive with Lessons 05-06.

Light-mode families (Lesson 04)

01 · Premium Utilitarian Minimalism

Smells like: Notion, Craft, Linear docs. A document pretending to be an app.

Substrate
Pure white #FFFFFF or warm bone #F7F6F3
Hierarchy
Editorial serif display (Lyon, Newsreader) over a clean sans (SF Pro, Geist, Switzer)
Edges
Exactly 1px solid #EAEAEA; radius 4-12px; shadows near zero (< 0.05 opacity)
Signature
Faux-OS window chrome, <kbd> keys, washed-out pastel tags, ink text #2F3437, gray #787774
Use when
Productivity tools, docs-like products, calm SaaS that must read as trustworthy and quiet

Defined by minimalist-ui (upstream: minimalist-skill)

02 · Editorial Luxury

Smells like: Kinfolk magazine, boutique hotels, high-end real estate, agencies.

Substrate
Warm cream #FDFBF7 with a subtle noise/film-grain overlay (~3-5% opacity)
Hierarchy
Massive high-contrast variable serif headings, tight tracking and line-height
Edges
Barely any: rules and whitespace, not boxes
Signature
Muted sage or espresso accents, small tracked caps for meta text, whitespace treated as the product
Use when
Lifestyle, real estate, print-adjacent brands, anywhere "considered" beats "efficient"

Defined by high-end-visual-design §3.A.2 (upstream: soft-skill)

03 · Soft Structuralism

Smells like: Apple Health, premium consumer wellness, polished portfolios.

Substrate
Silver-grey #F3F4F6 or plain white; cool, airy
Hierarchy
Massive bold grotesk (800 weight, tight tracking)
Edges
No borders. White components float on ultra-diffuse ambient shadows; radius 16-20px
Signature
Stat cards with big numerals, pill CTAs, everything breathing
Use when
Consumer health, personal metrics, friendly-premium products for a broad audience

Defined by high-end-visual-design §3.A.3 (upstream: soft-skill)

04 · Pristine Light

Smells like: quality print, Stripe's editorial pages, confident documentation.

Substrate
Off-white paper #FAF7F2, no texture tricks
Hierarchy
Sharp near-black sans with a strong size scale; numbered index rows
Edges
Hairline rules instead of cards; radius ~0-2px; no shadows
Signature
Reads like a well-set book page: editorial confidence, zero ornament
Use when
Content-first pages, methods, manifestos, pricing that wants to feel honest

Defined by imagegen-frontend-web §2, Theme Paradigm 1

05 · Quiet Premium Neutral

Smells like: Aesop, premium skincare, boutique spas, restrained luxury goods.

Substrate
Bone, sand, taupe, stone: #E9E4DB family, never white
Hierarchy
Light-to-regular weights, wide-tracked uppercase wordmarks, small sizes
Edges
Near-square (radius 2-3px), thin single-color borders on CTAs
Signature
Contrast lowered on purpose; the restraint is the brand
Use when
Premium goods where shouting would cheapen; note the a11y cost of low contrast, check body text still passes

Defined by imagegen-frontend-web §2, Theme Paradigm 4

Telling the lookalikes apart

PUM vs Pristine
PUM is an app (chrome, kbd keys, bordered blocks, pastel tags). Pristine is a page (rules, no containers, print energy).
Pristine vs Luxury
Both on paper tones. Luxury leads with a massive serif and whitespace; Pristine leads with a sharp sans and clarity.
Quiet vs the rest
Background turns stone or sand and contrast drops on purpose: you are in Quiet Neutral.
